About

Willa Potosnak is a incoming Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University, Robotics Institute. She is currently researching methods to improve machine learning (ML) model integrity and interpretability with the objective of ensuring the reliable implementation of artificial intelligence (AI) in practice. Among her research goals is to improve ML methods that are used to aid diagnostics and enhance people’s quality of life.

Research Intern in the Auton Lab

Willa currently works in the Auton Lab at Carnegie Mellon University under the mentorship of Dr. Artur Dubrawski and Dr. Kyle Miller.

Projects

Projects include both research as well as volunteer work.

Post-Cardiac Surgery Outcome Prediction

The objective of this research effort is to improve individual patient risk predictions for multiple outcomes, such as mortality and renal failure, following cardiac-surgeries, including coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG). The braoder inclusion of time-series intraoperative data in risk models to improve post-operative predictions has been one focus of the research effort.

RoboticsEd

RoboticsEd is a website of STEM resources developed with the goal of making robotics education more accessible to educators and their students. I am currently volunteering as a project lead of resource content and creation as well as website design for a team of students from the Robotics Community. This initiative is co-lead by Rachel Burcin and Dr. Maxim Likhachev.

Machine Learning Models with Intraoperative Features Improve Risk Predictions Following CABG

W. Potosnak, K. A. Dufendach, A. Kaczorowski, J. K. Miller, A. Kilic, A. Dubrawski

Risk models that identify patients likely to develop adverse outcomes following coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG)rely mainly on preoperative data. Machine Learning was used to determined whether inclusion of intraoperative data offers improved model risk predictions for multiple outcomes following CABG. Presented Society of Thoracic Surgeons (STS) 2022 Coronary Conference.

Robust Rule Learning for Reliable and Interpretable Insight into Expertise Transfer Opportunities

W. Potosnak, S. Caldas, K. A. Dufendach, J. K. Miller, A. Dubrawski

We propose an algorithmic rule selection approach which aims to select a short list of human-interpretable rules that reliably identify subpopulation beneficiaries of knowledge transfers in the form of machine learning risk models. In Proceedings of the Thirty-Sixth AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence.

Robust Interpretable Rule Learning to Identify Expertise Transfer Opportunities in Healthcare.

W. Potosnak, S. Caldas, K. A. Dufendach, J. K. Miller, A. Dubrawski

We aim to identify opportunities for beneficial transfer of knowledge across healthcare sites using a proposed algorithmic decision list rule selection approach within that is robust to sampling variance and yields reliable and human-interpretable insights into knowledge transfer opportunities. Bridging the Gap: From Machine Learning Research to Clinical Practice at NeurIPS 2021.

Graph Neural Networks for Improved El Nino Forecasting.

S. Rühling Cachay, E. Erickson*, A. F. C., Bucker*, E. Pokropek*, W. Potosnak*, Osei, S., & Lütjens, B

We propose the application of spatiotemporal Graph Neural Networks (GNN) to forecast ENSO at long lead times, finer granularity and improved predictive skill than current state-of-the-art methods. The explicit modeling of information flow via edges may also allow for more interpretable forecasts. Tackling Climate Change with Machine Learning workshop at NeurIPS 2020
